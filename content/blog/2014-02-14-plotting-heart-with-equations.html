Mathematical Valentine

It's Valentine's Day. It's that time of the year when many math geeks put their skills to creative use by drawing hearts with mathematical equations. For example, see this Mathematical Valentine on Reddit. One of the popular ways of plotting a heart I have seen discussed on the Internet is plotting a cardoid. For example, see this comment by on the same thread.

A cardoid is a curve traced by a point on the perimeter of a circle that rolls around a fixed circle of the same radius. See this Wikipedia article on cardiod for more details about it. Here is how it looks:

In fact, the cardoid is named so due to its heart-like shape. However, I wanted something that resembles the heart in playing cards more than it resembles a cardoid, so I plotted a simpler heart for my girlfriend. This simpler heart is composed of two simple curves: \begin{align*} y & = \sqrt{1 - |x|} \sqrt{|x|}, \\ y & = -\frac{3}{2} \sqrt{1 - \sqrt{|x|}}. \end{align*}

Here is what the graph plot looks like: heart-2014-02-14.png. Here is the source code for the above plot: heart.py.

In this post, I'll describe how I arrived at the two equations above.

Semicircles

We start with a circle of radius \( \frac{1}{2} \) centred at \( \left( \frac{1}{2}, 0 \right) \) and derive its equation: \begin{align*} & \left( x - \frac{1}{2} \right)^2 + y^2 = \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^2 \\ & \iff x^2 - x + y^2 = 0 \\ & \iff y^2 = x(1 - x). \end{align*}
